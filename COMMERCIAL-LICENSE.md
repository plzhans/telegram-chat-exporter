## When you do NOT need a commercial license

- Backing up your own chats, for any reason including work.
- A company running this internally for its staff, unmodified.
- Reading, studying, forking, or contributing to the code.
- Self-hosting the unmodified build on your own domain.

In short: using it is free. The AGPL asks nothing of you until you *modify* it and *offer it to
others over a network*.

## When you DO need one

The AGPL says that if you modify this code and let other people use it over a network, you must
offer them your modified source. If you want to do any of the following **without publishing your
changes**, you need a commercial license:

- Running a modified version as a public or customer-facing service.
- Bundling it into a proprietary product you distribute or host.
- Rebranding it as your own offering.

## How to get one

Open an [issue](https://github.com/plzhans/telegram-chat-exporter/issues) or contact the author
through their [GitHub profile](https://github.com/plzhans). Describe what you want to build and
we will work out terms.

The copyright is held solely by the author, so a commercial license can be granted directly
without involving other contributors.
